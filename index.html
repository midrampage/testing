Permits and Local Regulations

In New York, permit requirements vary by municipality:

  • Public Property Placement: If you need to place a dumpster on a public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way, you'll typically need a permit. For example, in Saratoga Springs, a Right of Way Permit from the Code Enforcement office is required, while the Village of Great Neck Estates requires a separate permit for each dumpster placed on public property.

  • Private Property Placement: If the dumpster will be placed entirely on your private property (like a driveway), permits are usually not required.

Don't worry about navigating complex regulations alone! We help determine if permits are needed based on your dumpster location and can guide you through the application process. In some cases, we can even handle the permit application for you.

We strictly adhere to New York State Department of Environmental Conservation (DEC) regulations and local waste disposal laws. Our team stays updated on all current requirements to ensure full compliance and environmental responsibility.
